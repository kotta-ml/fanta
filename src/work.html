.how to make a fictional character


First of all, what is a character sheet?

It is a sheet on which the appearance of a character and profile are put.

There aren't absolute rules about how to draw character sheets, but I think at
least you should draw a whole body of the character and close-up of his
or her face beside it.

In addition, you should write his or her profile including his or her name, age, job, or students, personality, special notes near by the pictures.


People tend to think the charm of pictures equals to the charm of story, however, the biggest factor of the charm of the story is the fascination of stories including attractive characters.

In this case, "characters attractiveness" refers to not just the charm and uniqueness of their appearances but also, more importantly, their
personalities.

As you may know, many of art and fictional creators and authors, before they
work on drawing pictures, they have to discuss the stories and characters
with editors seriously whether they are attractive enough or not;

The character they will portray. What will suit them better. Would it be better
to smack a pleasing looking character that is a trash on the inside trope on
the reader's face or startle them with a twist: the good guy is the bad guy.

Characters personality held great importance on the charm of the stories,
also at how they were portrayed. Because, from their experiences, they
know that those pictures are not just excellent visuals.

Let's say this character was cute. That's all? She's naive.
Wasn't everyone else? Is there something else? Please?



People don't always have to consider personalities of characters so seriously when you create them. If things are too demanding for beginners, they tend to lose interest.

However, intermediate or advanced would-be manga artists should do so. Because important characters' personalities and behavior including the main character's one are important facts to attract a large number of readers.

If there are only several words to express your main characters' personalities, this means you have not made them stand out as characters.

"What sort of hero is he?"
"He's brash."

That's not enough.

The hero brashness must have a root and reason. What drove him to be that way? Is he like that since he was a child? Do he have morality aside from that?

If you a make a story without establishing the characters' personalities, the readers can't understand what kind of persons they are while reading it.

Usually people are seldom charmed by characters whose personalities are unclear and hard to grasp. Before all, you would be at a loss when you create stories because you can't imagine how the characters behave in them.

He's showing malice. Do the hero frequently display malice on that face?
Is it just now? The hero is a level-headed man, so, what triggered him to stumble on such intention? Was it there at the very start?

Everyone is the main character of their story they say. It is true. But sometimes, there is better main character out there. And that is, the
obstacle, the rival.

I recommend you to device methods to create well-defined, impressive, and attractive characters that would fit on your story, not just that they exist.
